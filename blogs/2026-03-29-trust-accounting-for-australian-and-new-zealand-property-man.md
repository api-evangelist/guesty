<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-is-trust-accounting-and-why-does-it-matter-for-short-term-rental-managers"><strong>What is trust accounting, and why does it matter for short-term rental managers?</strong></h2>



<p>Trust accounting is the legally mandated practice of keeping owner funds separate from your own business revenue. In Australia — governed by legislation like Queensland&#8217;s Agents Financial Administration Act 2014, which regulates how agents establish, manage, and audit <a href="https://www.reiq.com/articles/property-management/trust-account-principles-for-principals">their trust accounts</a> — licensed property managers must maintain strict records of every dollar that flows through trust accounts. That includes guest payments, owner disbursements, commissions, and expenses.</p>



<p>Get it wrong, and you&#8217;re not just dealing with angry owners. Failure to comply can result in <a href="https://outbooks.com.au/guide-to-real-estate-trust-account-audits/">penalties, fines, or licence suspension</a>. The stakes are high, and the margin for error is zero.</p>



<p>For short-term rental operators juggling dozens or hundreds of properties across multiple channels, the complexity multiplies fast. Australia&#8217;s short-term rental market is valued at approximately AUD 3.4 billion and is projected to reach <a href="https://blog.madecomfy.com.au/resources/research/brisbane-short-term-rental-market-report">AUD 6.4 billion by 2030</a>. As of February 2026, STR listings alone reached <a href="https://switchsolutions.com/short-term-rental-growth-australia-forecast/">170,000 across Australia</a>. Every one of those bookings generates transactions that need to be tracked, categorised, and reported in regulatory format — accurately and on time.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-was-this-built-specifically-for-australia-and-new-zealand"><strong>Why was this built specifically for Australia and New Zealand?</strong></h2>



<p>This isn&#8217;t a global accounting module with a few regional settings toggled on. Guesty&#8217;s trust accounting solution was developed in collaboration with one of Australia&#8217;s top trust accounting audit firms, combining nearly 30 years of local expertise with global product development capabilities.</p>



<p>That expertise matters because every state handles trust accounting differently. NSW and Victoria run their audit period from 1 July to 30 June with reports due by 30 September, while Queensland operates on a 1 April to 31 March cycle with reports due by 31 May, and Western Australia runs a calendar year with a 31 March deadline. Guesty is built to meet QLD trust accounting compliance — one of the most demanding regulatory frameworks in the region. From ABA file generation to pre-formatted audit report packages, every ANZ-specific feature is built to match how compliance actually works on the ground.&nbsp;</p>
