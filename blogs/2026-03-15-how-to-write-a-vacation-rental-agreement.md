<p>Renting a property involves expensive assets and unpredictable human behavior. A handshake deal or a simple confirmation email leaves you exposed to noise complaints, property damage, and payment disputes. A written contract sets clear boundaries, protects your margins, and ensures operational control. This guide shows you how to build a vacation rental agreement that secures your business and your assets.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-tl-dr">TL;DR</h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Define exact check-in and check-out times to avoid occupancy gaps.</li>



<li>List every guest by name to prevent unauthorized parties or over-occupancy.</li>



<li>Specify house rules regarding noise, smoking, and pets to protect neighbor relations.</li>



<li>Detail your cancellation and refund policy tiers clearly.</li>



<li>Require a digital signature before granting property access.</li>



<li>Connect your agreement to your short term rental management software for automated delivery.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-identify-the-parties-and-the-property">Identify the parties and the property</h2>



<p>Start with the basics. Your <a href="https://www.guesty.com/blog/short-term-rental-agreement-guide/">agreement</a> must clearly state who is renting and what exactly they are renting. Use the full legal name of the primary guest. List the physical address of the property. Do not rely on &#8220;The Blue House&#8221; or &#8220;Unit 4.&#8221; Accuracy here prevents confusion during a dispute.</p>



<p>Specify the maximum occupancy. If your property sleeps six, state that only six people are allowed on the premises at any time. Guests often assume they can invite friends over for dinner. Define if &#8220;guests of guests&#8221; are permitted to prevent overcrowding and furniture wear.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-define-the-stay-period-and-access">Define the stay period and access</h2>



<p>Unused time is lost revenue. State the exact date and hour for check-in and check-out. You need these gaps for your cleaning team to perform a proper turnover. If a guest stays an hour late, it disrupts the entire turnover schedule. Mention any fees associated with unauthorized late check-outs.</p>



<p>“Strict check-out times protect your cleaning window, but for rare situations like a flight delay, a small grace period can prevent a one-star review. Balance your policy with common sense.”</p>



<p>Specify how the guest will access the property. Will you use a smart lock? Is there a physical key? Do not provide the access code until the agreement is signed. This is your primary point of control.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-establish-clear-house-rules">Establish clear house rules</h2>



<p><a href="https://www.guesty.com/blog/vacation-rental-house-rules-template/">House rules</a> turn verbal expectations into contractual obligations. Be specific about noise. Use phrases like &#8220;Quiet hours start at 10 PM.&#8221; This gives you legal ground to remove a guest if the neighbors complain about a loud party.</p>



<p>Smoking policies must be absolute. Specify if smoking is prohibited on the entire property or only inside. Mention the exact cleaning fee for odor removal to cover restoration costs. If you allow pets, define the breed or weight limits. Include a pet fee to cover the extra cleaning required for pet hair and dander.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-outline-financial-terms-and-cancellation-policies">Outline financial terms and cancellation policies</h2>



<p>Your agreement must match the financial data in your booking system. List the total rental amount, cleaning fees, and any local taxes. Be clear about when payments are due. If you require a deposit, state how and when it will be returned.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-use-a-vacation-rental-agreement-template">Use a vacation rental agreement template</h2>



<p>Use this structure as a starting point. Consult a local attorney to ensure it complies with your specific regional laws.</p>



<p><strong>Section 1: The parties</strong> This agreement is between [Your Name/Business] and [Guest Name]. Property Address: [Full Address]. Maximum Occupancy: [Number] people.</p>



<p><strong>Section 2: The term</strong> Check-in: [Date] at [Time]. Check-out: [Date] at [Time].</p>



<p><strong>Section 3: Fees and payment</strong> Total Rental Amount: [Amount]. Cleaning Fee: [Amount]. Security Deposit/Damage Protection: [Details].</p>



<p><strong>Section 4: House rules</strong></p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>No smoking inside the premises.</li>



<li>Quiet hours are from 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM.</li>



<li>No parties or unauthorized events.</li>



<li>Pets are [Allowed/Not Allowed].</li>
</ol>



<p><strong>Section 5: Liability</strong> The guest agrees to indemnify the host for any injuries or damages occurring during the stay.</p>



<p><strong>Section 6: Signatures</strong> [Host Signature] [Date] [Guest Signature] [Date]</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-automate-the-agreement-workflow">Automate the agreement workflow</h2>



<p>Do not wait for a problem to occur before you start using agreements. Send the contract immediately after a booking is confirmed. If you use vacation rental management software, set up an automated message flow. This ensures no guest enters your property without agreeing to your terms.</p>



<p>A cleaner finishes a same-day turnover without a checklist. Missing items surface in the next guest’s review because the expectations were not documented. Use your agreement to reinforce what is provided. If you do not provide beach towels, state it clearly.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-faq">FAQ</h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-is-a-digital-signature-legally-binding">Is a digital signature legally binding?</h3>



<p>Yes. In most jurisdictions, digital signatures carry the same weight as ink on paper. Use a platform that tracks the IP address and timestamp of the signature for extra security.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-should-i-use-an-agreement-for-airbnb-guests">Should I use an agreement for Airbnb guests?</h3>



<p>Yes. While Airbnb has its own terms of service, those terms are designed to protect the platform. Your private agreement protects you. It allows you to enforce specific rules like noise levels and local occupancy laws that the platform might not cover in detail.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-how-do-i-handle-a-guest-who-refuses-to-sign">How do I handle a guest who refuses to sign?</h3>



<p>Do not give them the keys. If a guest refuses to sign a standard agreement, it is a major red flag. Most professional hosts include a clause in their listing stating that a signed agreement is a requirement for the stay.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-can-i-charge-for-extra-guests-found-on-property">Can I charge for extra guests found on property?</h3>



<p>Only if your agreement says so. Explicitly state the &#8220;per person, per night&#8221; fee for any guests exceeding the agreed-upon number. This gives you the leverage to collect additional revenue if the guest brings extra people.</p>
